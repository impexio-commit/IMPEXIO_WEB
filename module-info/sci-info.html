What is a Sample Courier Invoice?

The Essential Document for Every Sample Shipment

A Sample Courier Invoice is a commercial document required by customs authorities when sending product samples internationally via courier (DHL, FedEx, UPS, Aramex etc.). It declares the contents, their value, origin and destination — even if the samples are sent free of charge.

Without a proper Sample Courier Invoice, your sample shipment can be held at customs, returned, or attract unnecessary duties and penalties. Many exporters lose valuable business opportunities because their samples didn't reach the buyer on time due to incorrect or missing documentation.

IMPEXIO's Sample Courier Invoice module generates a fully compliant, professionally formatted invoice with all fields required by international couriers and customs — Consignee, Notify Party, Port of Loading, Port of Discharge, Vessel/Flight No., Pre-carriage, Delivery Terms, Payment Terms, Marks & Nos., GRS/NET weight, and complete product line items with Amount in Words.

All Fields

Every Field — Explained

30+ fields organised across 3 clear steps — all required for a customs-compliant sample courier invoice.

📋 Step 1 — Header Information
🔢
Invoice No. Required

Unique invoice number auto-suggested as SCI/2026/XXXX. Required by couriers and customs for tracking and reference.

📅
Invoice Date Required

Date of invoice issuance — auto-filled with today's date. Must match the courier booking date.

🏢
Consignee (4 Address Lines)

Full name and address of the person or company receiving the samples. Courier delivers to this address. Shown in teal-bordered box on printout.

🔔
Notify Party (4 Address Lines)

Party to be notified when the shipment arrives — often the buyer's customs agent, freight forwarder, or a different contact than the consignee.

🌏
Country of Origin

Country where the sample products were manufactured. Required for customs to determine applicable duties and trade agreements.

🎯
Country of Final Destination

The final country where samples will reach. May differ from the port of discharge if there is onward movement.

⚓
Port of Loading

The airport or port from which the courier will depart India — e.g. Ahmedabad Airport, Mumbai Airport, NMIA.

🛬
Port of Discharge

The destination airport or port — e.g. Frankfurt Airport, Dubai International, JFK New York.

🏁
Final Destination

Specific city or address of final delivery if different from port of discharge.

✈️
Vessel / Flight No.

Courier flight number or vessel name — provided by DHL, FedEx etc. at time of booking. Required for customs tracking.

🚛
Pre-carriage By

Mode of transport used to bring samples to the airport or port — Road, Rail, or handed directly to courier.

📍
Place of Receipt by Pre-carriage

The location from where the courier collected the samples — factory address, CFS or warehouse.

🚚
Delivery Terms

Terms of delivery for the sample shipment — e.g. DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid), DDP (Delivered Duty Paid), EXW.

💳
Payment Terms

How the courier charges are paid — "Freight Prepaid", "Freight Collect" or "Account No. XXXXX".

📜
Inco Terms

International trade term applicable to the sample shipment — CIF, FOB, EXW etc.

💬
Other Terms

Any additional terms — e.g. "For Sample Purpose Only – No Commercial Value", "Not for Resale".

🏷️
Marks & Nos. / Kind of Pkgs.

Markings on the outer packaging — carton number, shipper's marks, handling instructions. Required by customs.

⚖️
GRS. WT. / NET WT.

Gross weight and net weight of the sample shipment in KGS. Required by the courier for freight calculation.

📦 Step 2 — Product Lines
📝
Product Description

Description of each sample item being sent. Be specific — include material, size, colour if applicable.

🔖
HS Code

Harmonised System tariff code for each sample product. Customs will use this to classify the goods.

🔢
Qty & Rate

Quantity of each sample and declared rate per unit. For samples, a nominal value is often declared — e.g. $2 per piece.

💲
Amount (Auto)

Amount = Rate × Qty, automatically calculated for each row. TOTAL sums all line amounts at the bottom.

🔡
Amount in Words (Auto)

Total invoice amount automatically converted to words. Required on courier invoices for customs — e.g. "Thirty One US Dollars and Fifty Cents Only".

✍️ Step 3 — Remarks & Authorization
🔗
Other Ref.

Reference number linking this sample invoice to a quotation, purchase order or buyer inquiry — e.g. "Ref: EQ/2026/0018".

💬
Remarks

Additional notes — e.g. "Samples sent against buyer request dated 01/03/2026", "No commercial value – for approval only".

👤
Prepared By & Authorised Signatory

Names that appear on the signature section — "For IMPEXIO / Authorised Signatory" format on the printed invoice.

Regulations

Country-Wise Sample Import Regulations

Different countries have different duty-free limits for sample imports. Know the rules before you ship.

🇺🇸
United States (USA)

Samples with a declared value under $800 can enter duty-free under the De Minimis threshold. Above this, formal entry and duties apply. Always declare actual value.

Duty-free: Under $800
🇪🇺
European Union (EU)

Samples with a value under €150 enter duty-free. Above €150, customs duties apply. VAT is charged regardless of value. Mark packages "Trade Samples – No Commercial Value".

Duty-free: Under €150
🇦🇪
UAE & GCC Countries

Samples of negligible value (AED 1,000 or less) are generally admitted duty-free if clearly marked as samples. Commercial samples require full documentation including an invoice.

Duty-free: Under AED 1,000
🇬🇧
United Kingdom (UK)

Post-Brexit, the UK follows its own rules. Samples under £135 enter duty-free. Above £135, customs duty and VAT apply. A commercial invoice is required for all shipments.

Duty-free: Under £135
🇨🇳
China

China has strict customs regulations. Samples must be declared at actual value. Items declared as "no commercial value" are often scrutinised. A proper invoice with HS codes is essential.

Declare actual value always
🌍
All Countries — General Rule

Regardless of value, all international sample shipments require a commercial invoice with HS codes, declared value, country of origin, consignee details and shipper signature.

Invoice always required
