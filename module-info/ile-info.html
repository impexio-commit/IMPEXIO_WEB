Overview

What is an Import Letter For Enquiry?

The first formal communication between an importer and a potential overseas supplier — setting the tone for the entire trade relationship.

An Import Letter For Enquiry (ILE) is a formal written communication sent by an importer to a prospective overseas supplier to gather product information, pricing, availability, and terms before placing a purchase order.

It serves as the official opening of trade negotiations — establishing your company's professional credibility and clearly communicating your import requirements. A well-drafted enquiry letter reduces back-and-forth, avoids misunderstandings, and accelerates the supplier response.

IMPEXIO's ILE module generates a print-ready, professionally formatted letter in minutes — covering all the essential information a supplier needs to send you a meaningful quotation or proforma invoice.

📩 Key Information Requested
💰Price list / CIF rate per unit
📦Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)
⏱️Lead time & delivery schedule
💳Payment terms & banking details
📋Product catalogue & specifications
🧪Sample availability & cost
🚢Port of loading & incoterms
Letter Structure

What the Letter Covers

Every section of the letter is structured to give the supplier exactly what they need to respond with a proper quotation.

🏢
Buyer / Importer Details
Your company name, address, contact person, GST number and reference — so the supplier knows exactly who is enquiring.
🌏
Supplier / Manufacturer Details
Full name and address of the overseas supplier or manufacturer being contacted for the enquiry.
📦
Product Specification
Detailed product description, HS Code, required specifications like grade, size, finish, colour and quantity needed.
🚢
Shipment Requirements
Port of loading, port of discharge, incoterms (CIF/FOB/EXW), preferred container type and expected delivery timeline.
💳
Commercial Terms
Requested payment terms, currency preference, pricing basis (per unit / per MT / per CBM) and any special commercial conditions.
📋
Information Checklist
A structured list of specific items you want from the supplier — price list, MOQ, lead time, samples, certifications, packing details and more.
📅
Response Deadline
A clear requested date by which you need the supplier's response — ensuring urgency and keeping your procurement timeline on track.
📝
Remarks & Special Instructions
Any additional notes — quality certifications needed, country of origin requirements, specific testing standards or custom requests.
✍️
Authorization & Signature
Prepared by and authorised signatory fields with your company name — giving the letter official credibility and legal standing.

Use Cases

When Do You Need This?

The Import Letter For Enquiry is the starting point for any structured import process.

🆕
Approaching a New Supplier
When contacting an overseas manufacturer or trading company for the first time, a formal enquiry letter establishes your credibility and communicates your requirements professionally.
💰
Price Discovery & Comparison
Send identical enquiry letters to multiple suppliers simultaneously to collect comparable quotes — enabling side-by-side price and terms analysis.
📦
New Product Sourcing
When looking to import a new product category, the ILE helps you clearly define specifications and gather accurate information before committing to any order.
🔄
Annual Rate Revision
Send annual or seasonal rate enquiries to your existing suppliers to renegotiate prices, review updated MOQs and confirm current terms for the new financial year.

ILE vs Export Quotation (EQ)

Both are formal commercial documents — but they serve opposite directions in trade.

AspectImport Letter For Enquiry (ILE)Export Quotation (EQ)
DirectionBuyer → Supplier (Import)Seller → Buyer (Export)
PurposeRequest price & info from supplierOffer price & terms to buyer
Who Creates ItImporter / BuyerExporter / Seller
ContainsProduct specs, quantity needed, terms requiredPrice, MOQ, delivery, payment terms
ResultSupplier sends back a Quotation or PIBuyer places a Purchase Order
