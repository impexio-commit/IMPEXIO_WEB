What is an Export Quotation?

Your First Commercial Communication to a Buyer

An Export Quotation is the very first formal document an exporter sends to a potential overseas buyer. It communicates your price, product details, delivery terms, payment conditions and shipment terms — all in one professional document.

Unlike a Proforma Invoice (which confirms a confirmed order), a Quotation is sent at the inquiry stage — when a buyer asks "how much will this cost?". A well-structured quotation impresses buyers, shows professionalism, and directly impacts your conversion rate.

IMPEXIO's Export Quotation module includes everything a modern export quotation needs — Rate CIF per product, packaging details (packed dimensions, inner pack, master pack), container size, sample status, special instructions, delivery and payment terms. All in a clean 3-step wizard with a teal-accented professional printout.

All Fields

Every Field — Explained

25+ fields organised across 3 steps — Header Info, Product Lines, and Terms & Remarks. Every field you need, nothing you don't.

📋 Step 1 — Header Information
🔢
Quot. No. Required

Unique quotation reference number. Auto-suggested as EQ/2026/XXXX — fully editable to match your system.

📅
Date Required

Quotation date — auto-filled with today's date. Displayed on the printout as DD/MM/YYYY.

🛍️
Product

Main product category or name for this quotation — e.g. "Ceramic Tiles", "Spices", "Leather Goods".

🌍
Buyer Required

Name of the overseas buyer or company this quotation is addressed to.

🗺️
Country

Destination country of the buyer — shown on the quotation header alongside buyer name.

📦 Step 2 — Product Lines
📝
Product Description

Detailed product description for each line item — grade, finish, size, specification etc.

🔖
HS Code

Harmonised System tariff code for the product — required for customs classification in the destination country.

🔢
Qty

Quantity being quoted. Used to calculate the total amount per line item automatically.

💲
Rate CIF & Amount (Auto)

CIF rate per unit. Amount = Rate × Qty, auto-calculated. TOTAL row sums all line amounts at the bottom.

📜 Step 3 — Terms & Conditions
⚓
Port of Loading & Port of Discharge

Origin and destination ports — e.g. Mundra to Hamburg. Shown in the Terms & Conditions section of the printout.

📜
Inco Terms

Trade terms defining cost and risk transfer — CIF, FOB, CFR, EXW etc. Determines how the Rate is calculated.

🏁
Final Destination

The ultimate delivery city or location — may differ from the port of discharge.

🚚
Delivery Time

Lead time from order confirmation to shipment — e.g. "30 Days from PO". Buyers use this to plan their inventory.

🚢
Shipment Type

Whether the shipment will be FCL (Full Container Load) or LCL (Less than Container Load).

💳
Payment Terms

How and when payment is expected — e.g. "30% advance, 70% against BL copy" or "LC at sight".

⏱️
Quotation Validity

How long this price is valid — e.g. "15 Days". After this date, prices may change due to raw material or freight cost changes.

📦
Packaging / Labeling

Packing type and labeling instructions — e.g. "Single colour box, buyer's label", "Export standard packing".

🏗️
Container Size

Which container size has been considered for this quotation — 20', 40' GP or 40' HC.

📐
Packed Dimension

Dimensions of the packed carton in cm — L × W × H. Used by the buyer to calculate their storage space.

🔢
Total Inner Pack & Total Master Pack

Number of pieces per inner pack and per master carton — critical for buyer's order quantity planning.

⚖️
Packed Weight

Weight of each packed carton in KGS — used to calculate gross weight for freight and customs purposes.

🧪
Sample Status

Current status of product samples — e.g. "Sample not yet approved", "Sample approved on 01/02/2026".

⚡
Special Instructions

Any buyer-specific instructions or requirements to be noted on the quotation.

📋
Other Desc. (Pre-filled)

Pre-filled with standard disclaimer: "Testing Charges, Inspection Charges and Special Packaging Charges will be extra as applicable." Editable.

💬
Remarks

Any additional notes, conditions, or information to be included at the bottom of the quotation.

👤
Prepared By & Authorised Signatory

Names that appear on the signature section of the printed quotation.

EQ vs PI

Export Quotation vs Proforma Invoice — What's the Difference?

Both are pre-shipment documents but they serve different purposes at different stages of the export process.

Criteria Export Quotation (EQ) Proforma Invoice (PI)
StageInquiry / Pre-negotiationOrder confirmed / Pre-shipment
PurposePrice offer to potential buyerFormal confirmation of sale terms
Used for L/C?✗ No✓ Yes
Rate BasisRate CIF per unitRate per unit (any Incoterm)
Packaging Details✓ Yes — full packaging infoPartial (Net/Gross Wt, CBM)
Sample Status✓ Yes✗ No
Buyer/Seller AddressBuyer + Country onlyFull 4-line address for both
Validity Period✓ Yes — standard fieldOptional
Amount in Words✗ Not required✓ Auto-generated
Print FormatA4 PortraitA4 Landscape
