What is a Proforma Invoice?

The First Step in Every Export Transaction

A Proforma Invoice (PI) is a preliminary invoice sent by an exporter to a prospective buyer before the actual shipment takes place. It is not a demand for payment — it is a formal quotation in invoice format that confirms the terms of the sale.

The PI includes the seller and buyer details, product descriptions, HS codes, quantities, unit rates, total amount, payment terms, shipment terms (Incoterms), port of loading, port of discharge, and validity period. The buyer uses it to open a Letter of Credit (L/C), arrange advance payment, or get import clearance approvals.

IMPEXIO's Proforma Invoice module guides you through a clean 3-step wizard — Header Info, Product Lines, and Remarks & Auth — so you never miss a field. Dynamic product rows, auto Amount in Words calculation, and professional A4 print output make it the fastest way to issue a PI.

All Fields

Every Field You Need — Nothing More, Nothing Less

The module covers all standard Proforma Invoice fields required for international trade — organised into 3 clean sections.

📋 Step 1 — Header Information
🔢
PI No. Required

Unique Proforma Invoice number. Auto-suggested as PI/2026/XXXX — editable to match your numbering system.

📅
Date Required

Invoice date — auto-filled with today's date. Change as needed.

🏭
Exporter / Seller (4 Address Lines)

Full name and address of the exporting company — shown on the left side of the printed invoice.

🌍
Buyer / Importer / Consignee (4 Address Lines)

Full name and address of the overseas buyer or consignee — shown on the right side of the printed invoice.

🌏
Country of Origin

Country where the goods are manufactured or produced. Required for preferential duty and certificate of origin.

🎯
Country of Final Destination

The final country where goods will be delivered — may differ from the port of discharge.

⚓
Port of Loading

The port from where goods will be shipped — e.g. Mundra, JNPT, Pipavav, Chennai.

🚢
Port of Discharge

The destination port where goods will be unloaded — e.g. Hamburg, Jebel Ali, Singapore.

🚛
Pre-carriage By

Mode of transport used to bring goods to the port — Road, Rail, or Inland Waterway.

✈️
Vessel / Flight No.

Name of the vessel or flight number on which the goods will be shipped.

📜
IncoTerms

Trade term defining buyer/seller responsibilities — FOB, CIF, CFR, EXW, DAP, DDP etc.

🏁
Final Destination

The specific city or location within the destination country where goods are ultimately delivered.

💳
Payment Terms

How payment will be made — e.g. 30% advance, balance against BL copy, LC at sight.

🚚
Delivery Terms

Conditions of delivery — e.g. CIF Dubai Port, FOB Mundra, EXW Factory.

🔄
Transhipment & Partial Shipment

Whether transhipment and partial shipments are allowed or not allowed — important for L/C compliance.

⏱️
Lead Delivery Time & Validity P.I.

How long delivery takes and how long this Proforma Invoice is valid — e.g. 30 days delivery, 15 days validity.

📦 Step 2 — Product Lines
📝
Product Description

Full description of the product being exported. Can include grade, specification, brand name etc.

🔖
HS Code

Harmonised System Code — the international product classification code required for customs clearance.

🔢
Qty & Boxes

Quantity of goods and number of cartons/boxes. Both columns are included as they may differ.

💲
Rate & Amount (Auto)

Unit rate per item. Amount = Rate × Qty, calculated automatically for every row. Add unlimited rows.

🔡
Amount in Words (Auto)

Total invoice amount automatically converted to words in the Indian numbering system — Lakhs, Crores etc.

⚖️
Net/Gross Weight & Total CBM

Cargo weight and volume fields at the bottom of the product table — required for shipping documents.

✍️ Step 3 — Remarks & Authorization
📋
Remarks

Any additional notes, special conditions, packing instructions or terms to include on the invoice.

👤
Prepared By & Authorised Signatory

Name of the person who prepared the invoice and the authorised signatory — both appear on the printout.

Use Cases

Who Uses the Proforma Invoice Module?

Anyone who exports goods internationally needs a professional Proforma Invoice before the shipment is confirmed.

🏭
Exporters & Manufacturers

Issue a formal PI to overseas buyers to confirm product specifications, price, payment terms and shipment schedule before production or dispatch begins.

💳
Letter of Credit (L/C) Transactions

Banks require a Proforma Invoice to open an L/C. The PI must include all shipment terms, HS codes, port details and validity period for the L/C to be issued correctly.

📩
Advance Payment Requests

Send a PI to request advance payment from the buyer before production. It serves as the formal confirmation of the commercial terms agreed between both parties.

🌐
Import Licence & Clearance

Many countries require a PI for the buyer to apply for an import licence or foreign exchange approval before the shipment is dispatched from India.
