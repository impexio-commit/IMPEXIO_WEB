What is an Import Purchase Order?

The Official Document That Confirms Your Import

An Import Purchase Order (IPO) is a formal commercial document issued by a buyer (importer) to an overseas supplier (exporter) to officially confirm the purchase of specific goods at agreed prices and terms.

Unlike an enquiry letter which requests information, a Purchase Order is a legally binding commitment — it tells the supplier exactly what to produce, in what quantity, at what price, when and how to ship, and on what payment terms.

IMPEXIO's IPO module generates a professional, complete Purchase Order covering all 10 essential sections — from buyer and supplier details to product lines, shipment requirements, quality standards, and full commercial terms — in a print-ready format.

🛒 What the PO Confirms
📦Exact product & specifications
🔢Quantity ordered per item
💰Agreed unit price & total value
🚢Delivery terms & port details
📅Shipment & delivery deadline
💳Payment terms & currency
📋Quality & packing requirements
PO Structure

What the Purchase Order Contains

Every section of the PO is structured to eliminate ambiguity and give the supplier everything they need to fulfil the order correctly.

🏢
Buyer / Importer Details

Your company name, address, GST/IEC number, contact person and PO reference — establishing the legal identity of the buyer.

🌏
Supplier / Seller Details

Full name, address and contact of the overseas supplier being issued the Purchase Order.

📦
Product Line Items

Detailed product description, HS Code, ordered quantity, unit, agreed unit price and line total — with grand total amount and currency.

🚢
Shipment & Delivery Terms

Port of loading, port of discharge, incoterms (CIF/FOB/EXW), container type, shipment deadline and expected delivery date.

💳
Payment Terms

Payment method (TT/LC/DP), advance percentage, balance payment trigger, currency and banking details reference.

📋
Quality & Standards

Required quality certifications, inspection requirements, testing standards, country of origin requirements and compliance conditions.

📦
Packing & Labelling

Packing specifications, carton size, inner pack quantity, master pack details, labelling requirements and marks & numbers.

📄
Documents Required

List of shipping documents the supplier must provide — Commercial Invoice, Packing List, BL, COO, Test Reports, Phyto Certificate etc.

📝
Special Instructions

Any additional conditions — penalty for late shipment, amendment procedure, partial shipment allowance, force majeure clause.

✍️
Authorization & Signature

Authorised signatory and company stamp — making the PO a legally valid document with full binding authority.

Use Cases

When Do You Use a Purchase Order?

An Import Purchase Order is required for every confirmed import transaction — big or small.

🏭
Placing a Regular Import Order

For every confirmed import from an overseas supplier, issue a formal PO to legally commit to the purchase and give the supplier clear instructions to proceed with production and shipment.

🔄
Repeat / Blanket Orders

For suppliers you import from regularly, duplicate previous POs and update quantities and dates — saving time on recurring orders while maintaining full documentation.

📊
Multi-Product Orders

When importing multiple products in a single shipment, a PO consolidates all line items in one document — making it easy for the supplier to pack and invoice correctly.

🏦
Bank & LC Documentation

A formally issued PO is required when opening a Letter of Credit (LC) with your bank — it forms the base document for the entire LC transaction and must match the PI exactly.
