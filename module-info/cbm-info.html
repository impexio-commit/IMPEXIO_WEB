What is CBM?

Cubic Metre — The Backbone of Freight

CBM (Cubic Metre) is the standard unit of measurement used in international shipping to determine the volume of cargo. It is calculated by multiplying the Length × Width × Height of a package in metres.

Every shipping line, freight forwarder, and customs authority uses CBM to calculate freight charges, determine how many boxes fit in a container, and plan container loading. A wrong CBM calculation can lead to extra freight charges, booking the wrong container size, or cargo not fitting at all.

IMPEXIO's CBM module removes all manual calculation errors. You enter your dimensions and quantities — it computes everything instantly: total CBM, total CFT (cubic feet), boxes per CBM, and container utilisation percentage.

📊 Quick Reference
📦 20' Container Capacity ~25–28 CBM
📦 40' GP Container Capacity ~55–58 CBM
📦 40' HC Container Capacity ~65–68 CBM
📐 1 CBM equals 35.31 CFT
🧮 Formula (cm) L×W×H ÷ 1,000,000
🧮 Formula (inch) L×W×H ÷ 1,728
