What is the Export Follow-up Mail Module?

Turn Silence into Sales with Professional Follow-ups

In international trade, sending a quotation or proforma invoice is just the beginning. Most conversions happen not at the first contact but through consistent, professional follow-up communication.

The Export Follow-up Mail module helps exporters generate context-specific, professionally worded follow-up emails for every stage of the buyer journey — from following up on a sent quotation to checking on a pending payment, confirming shipment dispatch, or requesting post-delivery feedback.

Each mail is pre-structured with the right tone, reference details, and call-to-action — so your communication always looks professional, is sent on time, and keeps the buyer engaged throughout the export transaction.

Mail Types

8 Types of Export Follow-up Mails

Every stage of the export transaction has a dedicated, professionally worded follow-up mail template.

📋
Quotation Follow-up
After sending EQ / Proforma Invoice
Politely follow up on a sent Export Quotation or Proforma Invoice that hasn't received a response. References the original document number, date and key pricing.
📋 Ref: EQ/PI Number
🧪
Sample Follow-up
After dispatching product samples
Follow up on samples dispatched via courier — asking for the buyer's feedback, approval status and any revision requirements before the bulk order.
📦 Ref: Courier tracking no.
📦
Order Confirmation Follow-up
After receiving a Purchase Order
Acknowledge receipt of a buyer's purchase order, confirm acceptance, and provide a production / shipment timeline to set clear expectations.
🛒 Ref: Buyer's PO Number
💳
Payment Follow-up
When advance payment is pending
Professionally request the advance payment or balance payment as per agreed terms — with banking details, due date and order reference clearly stated.
💰 Ref: Invoice / PO Value
🚢
Shipment Dispatch Follow-up
After goods are dispatched
Notify the buyer that the shipment has been dispatched — with vessel/flight details, Bill of Lading number, ETA and a list of shipping documents being sent.
📄 Ref: BL / AWB Number
📬
Documents Follow-up
After sending shipping documents
Confirm that all shipping documents (Invoice, Packing List, BL, COO, etc.) have been sent and request acknowledgement of receipt.
📋 Ref: Document set list
⭐
Delivery Feedback Follow-up
After buyer receives the shipment
Request feedback on the delivered goods — quality, packing, quantity accuracy and overall satisfaction — and invite the buyer to discuss their next order.
✅ Ref: PI / Invoice Number
🔄
Repeat Order Follow-up
To revive inactive buyers
Re-engage buyers who haven't ordered recently — share new product updates, revised pricing or seasonal offers to bring them back into the sales pipeline.
📈 Ref: Last order / New offer
Follow-up Strategy

The Ideal Export Follow-up Timeline

A structured follow-up sequence that keeps your buyer engaged from first contact to repeat order.

📋
Day 0 — Send Quotation
Export Quotation / Proforma Invoice Sent
Send the EQ or PI along with a courteous covering email. Set the tone for a professional relationship from the very first mail.
📧
Day 3–5 — First Follow-up
Quotation Follow-up Mail
If no response, send a polite follow-up referencing the quotation number. Ask if they received it and if they need any clarification.
🧪
Day 7–10 — Sample Stage
Sample Dispatch + Follow-up
If the buyer requested samples, dispatch them and send a follow-up mail with courier tracking details. Follow up again after 7 days for feedback.
💳
After PO — Payment Follow-up
Advance Payment Request
Once the PO is received, send a payment follow-up mail with your bank details and the advance amount due as per agreed terms.
🚢
After Dispatch — Shipment Update
Dispatch Confirmation Mail
Notify the buyer immediately when goods are dispatched — include vessel name, BL number, ETD, ETA and a list of shipping documents to follow.
📬
After Dispatch — Documents
Shipping Documents Sent
Confirm courier or email of original shipping documents and request acknowledgement. Ensure buyer has all documents needed for customs clearance.
⭐
After Delivery — Feedback
Delivery Feedback Request
Once the buyer receives the goods, send a feedback mail. Ask about quality, packing and satisfaction. This opens the door to the next order.
🔄
30–60 Days Later
Repeat Order Follow-up
Re-engage the buyer with a repeat order follow-up — share new products, seasonal pricing or special offers to bring them back for another order.

Pro Tips

Follow-up Best Practices

⏱️
Follow up within 3–5 days

Don't wait too long after sending a quotation. 3–5 business days is the ideal first follow-up window — the buyer still remembers your product.

📋
Always reference the document

Include the EQ/PI/BL reference number in every follow-up. Buyers deal with multiple suppliers — clear references avoid confusion and build credibility.

🎯
One ask per email

Each follow-up mail should have one clear call-to-action — confirm the order, make the payment, send feedback. Multiple asks dilute the response rate.

🌍
Mind the time zone

Send follow-up mails at the start of the buyer's business day in their timezone — this significantly improves open rates and response probability.
