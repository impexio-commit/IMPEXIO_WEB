What is Export Costing?

Your Internal Price Calculator Before Every Quote

Export Costing is the process of calculating the total cost of exporting goods — from the factory gate (EXW) to the buyer's destination (CIF). Before you quote a price to an overseas buyer, you must know exactly how much it costs to get your goods on a ship and to the buyer's port.

Without proper costing, exporters either quote too low (losing money) or too high (losing the order). IMPEXIO's Export Costing module is a confidential internal cost sheet — it is never shared with the buyer. It is used purely for calculating your break-even price and profit margin.

The module covers three cost stages: Local Transportation (getting goods to port), Custom Clearance & Handling (all port charges), and calculates your exact FOB value (your selling price at port) and CIF value (including marine insurance and sea freight to buyer's port) — in both INR and USD, across 3 shipment columns simultaneously.

How It Works

3 Steps to Your Complete Export Cost Sheet

A guided wizard walks you through document setup, the full costing sheet, and summary — no formulas needed, everything calculates automatically.

1
📋
Document Info

Enter Company name, Costing Ref. No., Product name, Rate On Date, USD exchange rate (1$ = INR) and Port of Loading. The USD rate powers all automatic currency conversions.

2
🧮
Costing Sheet

Fill in all cost inputs across 3 columns (LCL FCL, LCL LCL, 40' HQ FCL). Enter MOQ details, local transport charges, custom clearance fees, other expenses, marine insurance and sea freight. All totals and FOB/CIF values calculate live as you type.

3
📊
Summary & Auth

Review the FOB & CIF summary card showing all 6 output values for the reference column. Add Prepared By and Authorised Signatory. Save for records or Print a clean internal cost sheet.
