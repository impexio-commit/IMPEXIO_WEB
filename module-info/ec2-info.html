What is Export Costing 2?

Deeper Cost Breakdown for Port-Level Accuracy

Export Costing 2 is the advanced version of the standard Export Costing module. While Export Costing 1 uses FCL and LCL columns with a simpler layout, Export Costing 2 provides a more detailed port-level breakdown — especially in the Custom Clearance section where charges like ENS, VGM, CFS and service tax are itemised separately.

The key difference is that Export Costing 2 is structured around LCL shipment costing with two separate LCL columns (for comparing different scenarios) plus one 40' HQ Container column. This makes it ideal for exporters who primarily do LCL shipments and need to compare costs across different LCL configurations.

Just like Export Costing 1, it is a confidential internal document — never shared with buyers. It gives you your precise break-even price before you quote, protecting your margins on every shipment.

How It Works

3 Steps to Your Detailed Cost Sheet

The same clean 3-step wizard as Export Costing 1 — with deeper port-level charge detail in Step 2.

1
📋
Document Info

Enter Company name, Costing Ref. No., Product name, Rate On Date, USD exchange rate (1$ = INR) and Port of Loading. The USD rate powers all automatic currency conversions across all 3 columns.

2
🧮
Costing Sheet

Fill in MOQ details, EXW rate, all local transport charges, custom clearance fees (including ENS and VGM as manual inputs), other expenses, marine insurance and sea freight — across all 3 columns. All totals and FOB/CIF values calculate live as you type.

3
📊
Summary & Auth

Review the FOB & CIF summary card showing all 5 key output values for the reference LCL column. Add Prepared By and Authorised Signatory names. Save the record or Print your internal cost sheet.

Features

Advanced Costing for LCL Specialists

Every feature is designed for exporters who need detailed port-level cost visibility before finalising their LCL or FCL export price.

⚡
Fully Automatic Calculations

CBM Total, 18% Service Tax (both sections), section totals, FOB INR, FOB $, FOB/KG, CIF INR and CIF $ — all auto-calculated live across all 3 columns as you type.

📊
Dual LCL Columns

Two separate LCL columns let you compare costs for different scenarios — e.g. different freight rates, different routes, or different port agents — side by side on one sheet.

🚢
ENS & VGM as Manual Inputs

ENS (USD 25) and VGM (USD 20) are manual input fields — enter your actual INR equivalent. These are included in the Custom Clearance total automatically.

💱
Dual Currency (INR + USD)

Enter one USD/INR rate in Step 1 and all dollar conversions happen automatically across all 3 columns — FOB $ and CIF $ always stay in sync.

💾
Save & Retrieve

All EC2 cost sheets are saved separately from EC1 records. Search by company or costing reference. Edit and reprint any previous calculation instantly.

🖨️
Professional Print Output

A4 Portrait printout with IMPEXIO branding, gold "Costing Sheet" header, full 3-column cost table with navy/teal/gold row styling, and signatory section.

Use Cases

Who Uses Export Costing 2?

Export Costing 2 is ideal for exporters who regularly ship LCL or need to compare detailed port-level costs across shipment scenarios.

🚢
LCL Exporters

Exporters who regularly ship Less than Container Load (LCL) can use the two LCL columns to compare costs across different freight forwarders, routes or port agents before booking.

💰
Rate Comparison & Negotiation

Use the two LCL columns to enter quotes from two different freight forwarders. Instantly see which option gives the lower FOB/CIF cost — and negotiate from a position of knowledge.

🏭
Small & Medium Exporters

SME exporters who don't always fill full containers rely on LCL shipments. EC2 gives them the same professional costing tool as large exporters — tailored to their shipment reality.

📈
Freight Cost Monitoring

Run a new EC2 cost sheet every quarter or when freight rates change. Compare with saved records to track how rising freight, ENS or VGM charges are impacting your export margins.
